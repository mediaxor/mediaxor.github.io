Sound is a vital part of any multimedia application. An embedded audio system, such as MP3 player or in car audio, integrates various applications of sound and presents them to user in different ways. In a typical new age car - there is music, voice activated navigation system, bluetooth connectivity with smartphone, alert sounds to indicate a component malfunction, etc. In fact, a car audio system hosts ten's of audio algorithms that control each of these sound related functions. Embedded audio product development is a multiple stage process involving concept, research and development, audio tuning and acoustic measurements.

Our trainings offer to build an understanding around these processes and look into future technologies related to audio. When a student completes these trainings he/she should be able to

  • Understand the fundamentals of multimedia engineering.
  • Understand basic principles behind multimedia systems.
  • Apply the acquired knowledge to find solutions for problems relating multimedia.
  • Choose multimedia as a field for specialisation in higher courses.

EMBEDDED AUDIO ENGINEERING

This training starts with a discussion about sound and audio signals. We shall discuss various components of an audio system to understand the big picture. We shall then explore how data is presented to the processor and software building blocks of an embedded audio system. Finally, we'll cover some details on how these blocks are being used in modern operating systems such as Linux and Android.

AUDIO SIGNAL PROCESSING

This training starts with a discussion about sound and audio signals. We shall discuss various components of an audio system to understand the big picture. We shall explore basic building blocks of a digital signal processing system and learn fundamental signal processing algorithms and their applications into multimedia audio. We'll finally cover some advanced audio algorithms and understand their deployment and use in commercial systems.
